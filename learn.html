Physical Violence

Physical violence includes any act causing bodily harm or injury. It can range from pushing and slapping to severe assaults and in extreme cases, homicide. Physical violence often leaves visible marks, but can also include actions that don’t leave immediate visible injuries.

Warning Signs

  • Unexplained bruises, cuts, or injuries
  • Frequent “accidents” explained with inconsistent stories
  • Wearing clothing inappropriate for the season to hide injuries
  • A partner shows excessive jealousy or possessiveness
  • Fear or anxiety when around specific individuals

Emotional Violence

Emotional violence includes verbal abuse, threats, constant criticism, humiliation, and other tactics aimed at controlling, intimidating, or belittling the victim. This form of violence can be subtle and is often dismissed, yet causes significant psychological harm over time.

Common Tactics

  • Constant criticism and belittling
  • Name‑calling and humiliation
  • Gaslighting and manipulation
  • Isolation from friends and family
  • Threats and intimidation
